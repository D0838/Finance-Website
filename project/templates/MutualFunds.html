Mutual Fund Schemes - Your Finance Company

Mutual Fund

A mutual fund is a type of financial vehicle made up of a pool of money collected from many investors to invest in securities like stocks, bonds, money market instruments, and other assets. Mutual funds are operated by professional money managers, who allocate the fund's assets and attempt to produce capital gains or income for the fund's investors. A mutual fund's portfolio is structured and maintained to match the investment objectives stated in its prospectus.

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

A SIP allows investors to invest a fixed amount regularly in a mutual fund scheme. This scheme helps in averaging the cost of investment and instills a disciplined approach to investing.

"Invest regularly, grow steadily."

SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan)

SWP enables investors to withdraw a fixed amount from their mutual fund investment at regular intervals. This scheme is useful for those who need regular income from their investments.

"Your regular income, our priority."

STP (Systematic Transfer Plan)

STP allows investors to transfer a fixed amount from one mutual fund scheme to another at regular intervals. This helps in managing risk and aligning investments with market conditions.

"Smart transfers for balanced growth."

Dividend Payout

In the Dividend Payout scheme, investors receive dividends from the mutual fund's earnings at regular intervals. This provides periodic income to the investors.

"Reap the rewards regularly."

Dividend Reinvestment

In this scheme, the dividends declared by the mutual fund are reinvested back into the scheme. This helps in compounding the returns over time.

"Let your dividends grow further."

Growth Option

Under the Growth Option, the profits made by the mutual fund are reinvested in the scheme itself. Investors do not receive any interim payouts, and the returns are realized only when the units are redeemed.

"Watch your wealth accumulate."
